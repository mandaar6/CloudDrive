# Consequences of Non-Compliance for CloudDrive

## Overview
Failing to meet compliance requirements exposes CloudDrive and its operators to financial, legal, and reputational consequences.

## Consequences by Framework

### GDPR Non-Compliance
- Fines up to €20 million or 4% of global annual revenue, whichever is higher.
- Data subjects can sue for damages from unauthorized data exposure.
- Source: https://gdpr-info.eu/art-83-gdpr/

### CCPA Non-Compliance
- Civil penalties up to $7,500 per intentional violation.
- Consumers can seek statutory damages of $100–$750 per incident.
- Source: https://oag.ca.gov/privacy/ccpa

### HIPAA Non-Compliance
- Fines range from $100 to $50,000 per violation, up to $1.9 million per year per violation category.
- Criminal charges possible for willful neglect.
- Source: https://www.hhs.gov/hipaa/for-professionals/compliance-enforcement/index.html

### SOC 2 Non-Compliance
- Loss of enterprise customers who require SOC 2 certification from vendors.
- Reputational damage and inability to sign contracts with regulated industries.

### General Consequences
- Data breaches leading to user loss of trust
- Forced product shutdown pending remediation
- Increased costs of retroactive security fixes vs. proactive compliance

## Risk Summary
| Risk Level | Scenario |
|------------|----------|
| High | Unencrypted file storage + breach → GDPR/HIPAA fines |
| Medium | No audit logs → SOC 2 failure → lost customers |
| Low | Missing privacy policy → CCPA penalty |
