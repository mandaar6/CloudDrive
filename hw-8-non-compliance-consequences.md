# Potential Consequences for Non-Compliance

This document outlines the severe business, financial, and legal liabilities CloudDrive could face if it fails to adhere to the required data privacy and security compliance frameworks.

## 1. Financial Liabilities

### GDPR Regulatory Fines
*   **Official Source:** [GDPR Article 83 (General conditions for imposing administrative fines)](https://gdpr-info.eu/art-83-gdpr/)
*   **Consequence:** The EU authorities possess the power to levy catastrophic financial penalties for failing to protect user data or honor the Right to Erasure. The fines for severe violations can reach up to **€20 million, or 4% of the firm's worldwide annual revenue** from the preceding financial year, whichever is higher. 

### CCPA Civil Penalties
*   **Official Source:** [California Civil Code Section 1798.155](https://oag.ca.gov/privacy/ccpa)
*   **Consequence:** The California Attorney General can fine non-compliant companies up to **$2,500 per unintentional violation** and **$7,500 per intentional violation**. Because these fines are levied *per user*, a data breach or privacy failure affecting 10,000 California residents could instantly result in a $75 million fine.

## 2. Legal Liabilities and Corrective Actions

### FTC Consent Decrees
*   **Official Source:** [FTC Act Section 5: Unfair or Deceptive Acts or Practices](https://www.ftc.gov/legal-library/browse/cases-proceedings)
*   **Consequence:** If CloudDrive misrepresents its security posture or mishandles user files, the FTC can issue a consent decree. This legally binds the company to 20 years of continuous, expensive, independent, third-party security audits. Executives can be held personally liable for future breaches.

### Class-Action Lawsuits
*   **Official Source:** [CCPA Private Right of Action (Section 1798.150)](https://oag.ca.gov/privacy/ccpa)
*   **Consequence:** Both CCPA and GDPR grant users a "Private Right of Action." If CloudDrive fails to implement baseline NIST or SOC 2 technical controls, and user files are subsequently leaked, users can form massive class-action lawsuits suing the company for statutory damages without needing to prove actual financial loss.

## 3. Operational & Business Consequences

### Loss of B2B Revenue (Future Scope / Opportunity Cost)
*   **Official Source:** [AICPA Trust Services Criteria (B2B Procurement Standards)](https://www.aicpa-cima.com/resources/article/trust-services-and-information-security)
*   **Consequence:** While CloudDrive is currently a pre-revenue product, failing to design for SOC 2 and ISO 27001 creates massive future opportunity cost. Modern enterprise companies strictly enforce vendor risk management. Without these certifications, CloudDrive is fundamentally blocked from selling enterprise licenses to corporate clients in the future.

### Forced Data Destruction
*   **Official Source:** [FTC Settlement with Everalbum (2021) - Requirement to Delete Models and Data](https://www.ftc.gov/legal-library/browse/cases-proceedings/192-3172-everalbum-inc-matter)
*   **Consequence:** If CloudDrive collects user data without proper, documented consent (violating GDPR Article 7), regulators do not just issue a fine. As demonstrated in the FTC's 2021 order against Everalbum, regulators can legally force the company to permanently delete its entire database and any machine learning models trained on that improperly collected data, effectively resetting the business to zero.
